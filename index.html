About This Tool

What is this tool?

This tool estimates your next lawn mowing date using weather data and the Modified Growing Degree Day (MGDD) model, rather than a fixed calendar schedule. Because grass grows based on accumulated heat — not the calendar — this approach adapts to your local weather conditions in real time. All settings are saved automatically in your browser and restored on your next visit.

Two modes

  • Real-time — Uses historical PRISM data plus a 15-day GFS forecast to estimate your next specific mow date. Shows an accumulation chart and day-by-day breakdown.
    • Extend with climate normals — Enable this toggle to project beyond the 15-day forecast window using 30-year PRISM averages. Extended rows appear in amber italics in the forecast table and as a separate line on the chart.
  • Climatology — Uses 30 years of PRISM normals (1991–2020) to show average mowing intervals throughout the year for your location. Useful for seasonal planning. Does not require a last mow date.

How to use it

  1. Select your location on the map (click, drag the marker, search, or use the geolocation button)
  2. Pick a grass type preset or set custom base/upper/target values
  3. Enter your last mow date
  4. Click Estimate Next Mow Date
  5. Switch between the Plot and Table views using the toggle below the results; use the maximize button to view the chart fullscreen

Understanding the science (MGDD)

Plants grow based on accumulated heat, not calendar days. Each day contributes heat units above a base temperature (below which growth stops). Modified GDD also caps daily contributions at an upper temperature where growth slows due to heat stress. When accumulated MGDD since your last mow reaches a target threshold, it's time to mow again.

Grass type presets

Selecting a preset fills in base, upper, and target MGDD values. It also sets species-specific dormancy-break thresholds used by growing season detection:

  • Kentucky Bluegrass (cool-season) — Base 32°F, Upper 86°F, Target 320 MGDD | Green-up: 200 GDD above 32°F, min 0.25 in rain/14 days
  • Bermuda Grass (warm-season) — Base 50°F, Upper 95°F, Target 225 MGDD | Green-up: 150 GDD above 41°F, min 0.50 in rain/14 days
  • Zoysia Grass (warm-season) — Base 41°F, Upper 86°F, Target 345 MGDD | Green-up: 175 GDD above 41°F, min 0.40 in rain/14 days

These are starting points; adjust to match your lawn's actual growth rate. Manually editing any value reverts the dropdown to "Custom."

Growing season detection

The tool detects when grass breaks spring dormancy using a layered guard-rail model. All five conditions must be met before mowing GDD begins to accumulate. Thresholds for the GDD and precipitation gates vary by species (see presets above).

  • Feb 15 biofix — No GDD accumulates before February 15. Meaningful turfgrass development does not occur before mid-February at any US latitude (matches MSU GDD Tracker).
  • Hard freeze reset — If a day's minimum temperature drops to 25°F or below after the biofix, accumulated green-up GDD resets to zero. Hard freezes kill newly de-hardened tissue, undoing dormancy-break progress.
  • Day-length floor — Season cannot start until daylight at your latitude exceeds 10 hours (a latitude-dependent astronomical constraint, not species-specific).
  • Cumulative GDD threshold — After the above gates pass, GDD accumulates using a species-specific base temperature. Season starts when the thermal sum reaches the green-up threshold.
  • Precipitation check — Minimum recent rainfall over the prior 14 days is required, since thermal accumulation alone cannot induce sustainable green-up without adequate soil moisture.

Precipitation and moisture

In real-time mode, the tool adjusts daily MGDD contributions based on recent rainfall. A rolling 7-day precipitation total is compared to each grass type's optimal water need. When rainfall is below optimal, daily growth contributions are scaled down (to a minimum of 50% — grass slows but rarely stops completely during growing season). When rainfall meets or exceeds the optimal amount, no reduction is applied.

  • Irrigated toggle — If your lawn is irrigated, enable this to bypass precipitation adjustments entirely (multiplier always 1.0).
  • Moisture status — After calculation, a status indicator shows current soil moisture conditions: adequate (green), slightly dry (yellow), or drought stress (red). Irrigated mode shows blue.
  • Precipitation probability — The forecast table and weather strip show the daily chance of rain (from Open-Meteo GFS). This column is hidden for climate normals rows and suppressed in irrigated mode.
  • Climatology mode does not apply precipitation scaling, since 30-year averages already incorporate typical precipitation patterns.

Results and mowing guidance

  • Mow window — The optimal mow window spans 90–110% of your target MGDD. The estimated mow date is when accumulation crosses 100%; mowing anywhere in this range is appropriate.
  • Weekend suggestion — If the estimated mow date falls on a weekday, the tool suggests the nearest weekend day and notes whether it falls within the mow window.
  • Weather strip — A 5-day strip centered on the mow date shows temperature, precipitation, wind, and weather icons. Alerts flag rain on mow day (with probability), heavy rain nearby, or high winds.
  • Mow history — Click Log Today as Mowed to record a mow date. History is stored locally in your browser and can be cleared at any time. Logging a mow updates the last mow date field automatically.

Settings and display

  • Unit system — Toggle between imperial (°F / in) and metric (°C / mm) using the buttons in the parameters panel. All internal calculations use imperial; the toggle converts displayed values on the fly.
  • Theme — Choose light, dark, or system-default using the buttons in the page header. Your preference is saved.
  • Settings persistence — Location, grass type, temperatures, last mow date, toggles, and theme are all saved to your browser's local storage and restored on reload.
  • Location — Click the map, drag the marker, or type an address in the search box. The geolocation button uses your browser's location API; if unavailable, it falls back to IP-based detection.

Data sources

  • Historical weather: ACIS PRISM GridData (Regional Climate Centers)
  • Forecast weather: Open-Meteo GFS (15-day forecast)
  • Map tiles: OpenStreetMap
