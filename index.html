About This Tool

What is this tool?

This tool estimates your next lawn mowing date using weather data and the Modified Growing Degree Day (MGDD) model, rather than a fixed calendar schedule. Because grass grows based on accumulated heat — not the calendar — this approach adapts to your local weather conditions in real time.

What are Growing Degree Days?

Plants grow based on accumulated heat, not calendar days. Each day contributes heat units above a base temperature (below which growth stops). Modified GDD also caps daily contributions at an upper temperature where growth slows due to heat stress. When accumulated MGDD since your last mow reaches a target threshold, it's time to mow again.

How to use it

  1. Select your location on the map (click, drag the marker, search, or use the geolocation button)
  2. Pick a grass type preset or set custom base/upper/target values
  3. Enter your last mow date
  4. Click Estimate Next Mow Date

Two modes

  • Real-time — Uses historical PRISM data plus a 15-day GFS forecast to estimate your next specific mow date. Shows an accumulation chart and day-by-day breakdown.
  • Climatology — Uses 30 years of PRISM normals (1991–2020) to show average mowing intervals throughout the year for your location. Useful for seasonal planning.

Growing Season Detection

The tool automatically detects when grass breaks dormancy in spring using the PACE Turf Growth Potential model (species-specific temperature response), preventing false mowing recommendations during winter warm spells.

Grass type presets

  • Kentucky Bluegrass (cool-season) — Base 40°F, Upper 90°F, Target 150 MGDD
  • Bermuda Grass (warm-season) — Base 50°F, Upper 100°F, Target 200 MGDD
  • Zoysia Grass (warm-season) — Base 50°F, Upper 95°F, Target 180 MGDD

These are starting points; adjust to match your lawn's actual growth rate.

Precipitation Adjustment

In real-time mode, the tool adjusts daily MGDD contributions based on recent rainfall. A rolling 7-day precipitation total is compared to each grass type's optimal water need. When rainfall is below optimal, daily growth contributions are scaled down (to a minimum of 50% — grass slows but rarely stops completely during growing season). When rainfall meets or exceeds the optimal amount, no reduction is applied.

  • Irrigated toggle — If your lawn is irrigated, enable this to bypass precipitation adjustments entirely (multiplier always 1.0).
  • Moisture status — After calculation, a status indicator shows current soil moisture conditions: adequate (green), slightly dry (yellow), or drought stress (red).
  • Climatology mode does not apply precipitation scaling, since 30-year averages already incorporate typical precipitation patterns.

Data sources

  • Historical weather: ACIS PRISM GridData (Regional Climate Centers)
  • Forecast weather: Open-Meteo GFS (15-day forecast)
  • Map tiles: OpenStreetMap
